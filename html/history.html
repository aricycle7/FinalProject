The History of This great City

Columbus was founded in 1812 as a political center. Columbus started growing in 1831 as a feeder branch of the Ohio River was opened and continued when the first railroad was opened in 1850. Columbus was an important city during the American Civil War, being home to one of the largest Prisoner Of War camps for Confederates, Camp Chase. After the Civil War the economy boomed along with industrial growth. Columbus became one of the largest transportation and commercial centers. After the Scioto River floods of 1913 the river was widened and riverfront development flourished. Columbus was still growing after World War 2 with many companies moving thier headquarters to the city.
